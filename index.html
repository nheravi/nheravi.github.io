Negin Heravi

I am a Senior Machine Learning Engineer at Snap working on face tracking and face detection systems in the Generative AI organization. My research interests are representation learning, multi-modal perception, computer vision, augmented reality, robotics, and haptics.

Previously, I completed my PhD at Stanford University where I worked on learning-based methods for multimodal object representations for virtual and augmented reality. I was part of the Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (SAIL), and a member of the IPRL and CHARM labs advised by Jeannette Bohg and Allison Okamura. My PhD was partially funded by the NSF GRFP. Prior to Stanford, I completed my undergraduate degree in Mechanical Engineering at MIT. I have also interned at Google Brain, Amazon, and Apple.

Research
Omni-ID: Holistic Identity Representation Designed for Generative Tasks
Guocheng Qian, Kuan-Chieh Wang, Or Patashnik, Negin Heravi, Daniil Ostashev, Sergey Tulyakov, Daniel Cohen-Or, Kfir Aberman
CVPR 2025
paper | project page

Product integrated, patent filed.

Omni-ID is a novel facial representation tailored for generative tasks, encoding identity features from unstructured images into a fixed-size representation that captures diverse expressions and poses.
Visuomotor Control in Multi-Object Scenes Using Object-Aware Representations
Negin Heravi, Ayzaan Wahid, Corey Lynch, Pete Florence, Travis Armstrong, Jonathan Tompson, Pierre Sermanet, Jeannette Bohg, Debidatta Dwibedi
ICRA 2023
paper | project page | video

We show the advantage of learned self-supervised object-aware representations obtained using Slot Attention for downstream robotic tasks. Our results show a 20% increase in performance compared to using raw RGB in low data regimes (1000 trajectories) in policy training using implicit behavioral cloning (IBC).

Development and Evaluation of a Learning-based Model for Real-time Haptic Texture Rendering
Negin Heravi, Heather Culbertson, Allison Okamura, Jeannette Bohg
IEEE Transactions on Haptics 2024
paper

We evaluate the performance of our learning-based action-conditional model for haptic texture rendering through human studies. Our model takes as input the GelSight image of a texture, human's speed and force, and renders the corresponding vibration of that texture in real time. For rendering, we use a high bandwidth vibrotactile transducer, called a Haptuator, attached to a 3DSystem's Touch device.

Predicting Hand-Object Interaction for Improved Haptic Feedback in Mixed Reality
M. Salvato, Negin Heravi, Allison Okamura, Jeannette Bohg
RA-L and ICRA 2022
paper

We show that it is possible to predict the timing of future hand-object interaction based on a history of hand poses. Our model takes as input a history of hand and object poses, and predicts upcoming contact for a horizon of 100 ms.

DiffImpact: Differentiable Rendering and Identification of Impact Sounds
Samuel Clarke, Negin Heravi, Mark Rau, Ruohan Gao, Jiajun Wu, Doug James, Jeannette Bohg
CoRL 2021 (Oral Presentation)
paper | project page | video

We present DiffImpact, a fully differentiable model for sounds rigid objects make during impacts, based on physical principles of impact forces, rigid object vibration, and other acoustic effects. Its differentiability enables gradient-based, efficient joint inference of acoustic properties of the objects and characteristics and timings of each individual impact.
Learning an Action-Conditional Model for Haptic Texture Generation
Negin Heravi, Wenzhen Yuan, Jeannette Bohg, Allison Okamura
ICRA 2020
paper | project page | dataset

We present a learned action-conditional model that uses data from a vision-based tactile sensor (GelSight) and user's action as input. This model predicts an induced acceleration that could be used to provide haptic vibration feedback to a user. We trained our proposed model on a publicly available dataset (Penn Haptic Texture Toolkit) that we augmented with GelSight measurements of the different materials. We show that a unified model over all materials outperforms previous methods and generalizes to new actions and new instances of the material categories in the dataset.
